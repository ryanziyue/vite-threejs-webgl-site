My Work

02

Here are some of my more recent projects:

  • CanSat

    — a sounding rocket payload created for the advanced category of the Canadian CanSat Design Competition. The TurkeyBurkey CanSat measures air temperature and pressure during freefall, and seismic activity upon landing on the ground. To measure seismic activity, a process researched by the Japanese Seismological Agency was adapted, using Fourier transforms to reduce system noise and improve measurement accuracy.

    Data is transmitted by LoRa radio through the Arduino Serial protocol to a ground station, where it is read by pySerial and parsed into an xlsx file to be graphed by matplotlib. The CanSat won the "Science Mission Award", awarded to the most well-researched and scientifically sound science mission.

  • Levelhead

    — a computer vision-based app to help improve basketball dribbling practice efficacy. Dribbling drills are most effective when the athlete's head is completely level, but this could be difficult to maintain when practicing by oneself. Levelhead calculates the angle the user is facing and issues reminders when the user is facing suboptimal angle for basketball dribbling drills.

    The entirety of Levelhead was built in python. Mediapipe was used to determine the points of the face necessary for the perspective-n-point calcuation in OpenCV. Levelhead won the "Best High School Solo Hack" as well as the "Overall Hackathon Winner" prizes.

  • Cardboard++

    — an accessible VR headset built on cardboard, arduino, and websockets for the vsHacks! hackathon. Cardboard++ converts the user's head tilt to corresponding mouse movements and displays computer video output on a mobile device to create a serviceable, budget VR experience.

    Pitch and yaw of the user's head is measured and converted to mouse movement using pyautogui. Meanwhile, computer video output is transformed to fit the VR video format and forwarded to a mobile device using a basic Node.JS site and the WebSocket protocol. Cardboard++ won the runner-up prize of the vsHacks! hackathon.

  • Artly

    — a social media concept developed for the WaffleHacks2022 hackathon. Users are able to create their own drawings on a TypeScript canvas and draw on top of the artwork of others. Drawings and their derivations are then recommended to users based on a platform-wide voting system, thus creating a platform where original artwork is constantly promoted and encouraged.

    The entirety of the site is built on Next.JS with TypeScript and styled with TailwindCSS. User data, containing account details and drawings, are stored using Firebase. Artly won the "Best Hack for Connections" award and "Honorable Mention in Art & Music".
